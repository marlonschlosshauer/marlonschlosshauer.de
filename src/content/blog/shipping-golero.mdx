# Shipping Golero

<Meta {...metadata} className="pb-4" />

Early in my career I joined a small team to build a soccer prediction app. The operation was very amature and the business failed. Shortly before pronouncing the project dead, I embarked on a redesign of the entire app. I got to use part of that redesign in [pitch](https://www.figma.com/design/Wg2c9sk6nqbeWrydDOxCMg/Portfolio--Tippinho?node-id=0-1&t=euCBXXiFDOqdV9dh-1), too, which I was very proud of at the time.

My goal was to trim down the surface area of the app as much as possible. Remove all of the fat. Focus on the main functions and make those as easy & fast as possible to use.

That redesign has been running around in my head for a couple of years now, so I thought why not build it myself. As it turns out, optimizing for simplicity, in combination with modern tools like Claude Code & Convex, made it a delight to implement.

The app is available to download on the app store: [Golero](https://apps.apple.com/app/6760223128). The Figma file is available [here](https://www.figma.com/design/Wg2c9sk6nqbeWrydDOxCMg/Portfolio--Tippinho?node-id=6-5924&t=VgC4hyVQZblLkrTo-4).

## Party trick

The core loop of the app is about entering predictions for upcoming matches. You receive points depending on how close you are to the actual result. I wanted to make that flow as fast and frictionless as possible. Here is a recreation of how it works in the app:

<PartyTrick />

All available matches are put into a slider. Predictions are only a single digits. Upon input, the focus immediatly jumps to the next possible input. Backspace goes moves the focus backwards.

Allowing the user to navigate with their keyboard speeds up the process immensily. Having users spent as little time as possible in the app is a key goal. Having people bounce around the app makes expectations grow and for an app that focuses on doing one thing well, being modest in scope is the perfect fit.

## Pit falls

-   Android not rendering text inside of a `<TextInput/>`
-   Transitions in React Native 0.81 not working as they should
-   Expo Router not having the concept of a `Loading.tsx`
-   Convex Auth has some rough edges

This was a reminder that APIs are the bottleneck. Claude can one-shot pretty much any of my simple requests. To the point where, I went through the app, made a TODO list of all the things I didn't yet like, and just fired them into Claude Code, queueing them up, one after another. I can also trivially steer it to do things how I would I would do them, with just a few lines in a `CLAUDE.md`.

However inconsistencies stop the entire operation in its tracks. You now need to figure out why it doesn't work and you need to fix it, without destroying what currently works.

This became painful when I finally embraced Convex. As backend functions became something Claude one-shotted in mere seconds, the UI components became the bottleneck. It felt weird spending minutes on getting a button to fit in the layout correctly while getting the API endpoint for an entire screen with live sync, done in a couple dozens of seconds.

## Deploying mobile apps is still annoying

Expo has made developing mobile apps so much easier. EAS has made deploying not make me want to jump off a bridge. It is still rough though. Feels like you're playing a turn-based quick-time event where at every turn something catastrophic might happen, all the while praying that the magic of EAS keeps the hell that is Xcode & App Store Connect away from you.
